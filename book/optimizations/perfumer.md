# Optimization: The Perfumer

**World**: The Perfumer (#103)
**Type**: Long-lasting (Genealogy: Ancient Egypt -> Grasse -> Synthetic Chemistry)

## 1. Value Systems Selection (from VALUES.md)

To optimize this world, we focus on:

*   **Phenomenological (Values)**: Olfaction. The sense most directly linked to the limbic system (memory/emotion). The Perfumer paints with invisible colors.
*   **Aesthetic of the Artificial (Aesthetic)**: The Synthetic. The realization that "Natural" is not always better. Aldehydes (Chanel No. 5) created a scent that does not exist in nature.
*   **Economical (Values)**: The Brand. Perfume is the highest margin product in the luxury world. Selling water and alcohol for the price of gold.

## 2. Intervention Techniques (from INTERVENTION_TECHNIQUES.md)

We apply these techniques to shift the world and reveal its core:

*   **Metaphor**:
    *   *The Organ*: The Perfumer's desk, arranged with hundreds of vials like the keys of a musical instrument. He composes "Notes" and "Chords."
    *   *The Ghost*: The scent is a ghost of a flower. It is presence in absence.

*   **Somatic Intervention**:
    *   *The Nose*: The "Nez." A professional athlete of smell. Insured for millions.

*   **Dialectics**:
    *   *Top/Base*: The volatility of the scent. The Top notes fly away; the Base notes stay. The perfume is a temporal sculpture.

He sits at the organ, surrounded by amber glass. He dips the blotter strip (*mouillette*) into the vial. He waves it under his nose. He pays attention to the **Silence**. He is listening for a smell.
