# Optimization: The Tea Ceremony

**World**: The Tea Ceremony (#94)
**Type**: Long-lasting (Genealogy: Zen Buddhism -> Sen no Rikyu)

## 1. Value Systems Selection (from VALUES.md)

To optimize this world, we focus on:

*   **Aesthetic of the Artificial (Aesthetic)**: *Wabi-Sabi*. The appreciation of the imperfect, the impermanent, and the incomplete. The cracked cup is more valuable than the perfect one.
*   **Phenomenological (Values)**: Mindfulness. The hyper-attention to the micro-movements of the ritual.
*   **Ethical (Values)**: Hospitality. The host exists entirely for the guest.

## 2. Intervention Techniques (from INTERVENTION_TECHNIQUES.md)

We apply these techniques to shift the world and reveal its core:

*   **Somatic Intervention**:
    *   *The Whisking*: The rapid back-and-forth motion of the bamboo whisk (*chasen*). It is a somatic mantra.
    *   *The Kneeling*: The *Seiza* position. Painful, grounding, humble.

*   **Temporal Shift**:
    *   *Ichigo Ichie*: "One time, one meeting." The realization that this specific gathering will never happen again. It creates an infinite value in the present moment.

*   **Metaphor**:
    *   *The Dewy Path*: The *Roji* (garden path) leading to the tea house. It is a transition zone to shed the dust of the world.
    *   *The Crawl Door*: The *Nijiriguchi*. A tiny door that forces everyone, even a samurai, to bow and crawl to enter. It is an equalizer.

## 3. Registers

### Formal Register
The Tea Ceremony (*Chanoyu*) represents a "Ritualized Consumption Event" designed to induce a state of "Shared Mindfulness." Originating in Zen monasteries, it was codified by Sen no Rikyu as a political and spiritual tool for the warrior class. The "Tea Room" is a "Deprived Sensory Environment" (minimalist decor, silence) that forces attention onto the specific objects (utensils) and actions (pouring, drinking). The tension lies in the "Ossification" of the practice into rigid schools (*Urasenke*, *Omotesenke*) versus the "Modern Casualization" (matcha lattes). The optimization is the recovery of the "Radical Presence" of the original intent.
