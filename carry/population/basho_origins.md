# Matsuo Bashō: Haiku Master 1680 - Assemblage Origins

*Speculative developmental history: how did a wandering poet's assemblages form?*

---

## PHASE 1: Samurai Youth in Iga (1644-1666)

### Origin Assemblages

**Low-Rank-Samurai-Son**
- Origin: Matsuo family, minor retainer class
- Grew into: Literacy, some education
- BUT: No high status, no wealth
- Later enables: Class escape via poetry

**Servant-to-Young-Lord** (Todo Yoshitada)
- Origin: Age ~9, page/companion to lord's son
- Grew into: Exposure to poetry culture
- Yoshitada wrote haikai → Bashō learned too
- **Crucial formation**: Poetry as social bond

**First-Haikai-Attempts** (age ~18)
- Origin: Imitating master Yoshitada
- Style: Playful, witty, crude (Danrin school)
- Not yet "Bashō" - just another versifier

**Master's-Death** (Yoshitada dies 1666)
- Origin: Patron/master/friend dies young
- **Assemblage destruction**: Purpose collapses
- Grew into: Masterlessness, wandering begins

**Rōnin-Become** (masterless samurai)
- Origin: No lord to serve after 1666
- Grew into: Freedom AND poverty
- Status: Declassed, but also unbound

---

## PHASE 2: Edo Period & School Formation (1672-1680)

### From Rōnin to Poet

**Edo-Migrant** (age ~28)
- Origin: Left Iga for capital after years of drift
- Grew into: Urban poet identity
- City assemblages: customers, patrons, rivals

**Haikai-Marker** (verse judge/corrector)
- Origin: Economic necessity, skill monetized
- Grew into: Teaching assemblage beginnings
- Income source: Judging others' poems for fee

**Danrin-School-Member**
- Origin: Dominant style of era, joined
- Playful, pun-heavy, popular haikai
- Later REJECTED for different path

**First-Disciples-Gathering**
- Origin: Teaching creates students
- Age ~35: Enough reputation for followers
- Grew into: Shōmon (Bashō school)

**Bashō-An-Naming** (1680)
- Origin: Disciple gifts banana plant (bashō)
- Takes name from plant → identity shift
- Hut by river, not city center
- Grew into: Recluse-poet assemblage

---

## PHASE 3: Transformation (1680-1684)

### The Break

**Bucchō-Zen-Training**
- Origin: Sought teacher, found Zen monk
- Grew into: Meditation practice, spiritual depth
- Injected into: Poetry (changes everything)

**Fire-and-Homelessness** (1682)
- Origin: Edo fire destroys Bashō-an
- **Assemblage destruction**: Home gone
- Grew into: Non-attachment practice
- Seeds: Later wandering, no fixed address

**Mother's-Death** (1683)
- Origin: Return to Iga, funeral
- Grew into: Mortality awareness, transience theme
- Poetry deepens

**First-Major-Journey** (1684)
- Origin: Grief, restlessness, seeking
- Grew into: Wandering-Writer assemblage
- Travel + poetry fuse: haibun form

**Danrin-Rejection** (gradual)
- Origin: Dissatisfaction with witty but shallow
- Grew into: Sabi aesthetic, loneliness, depth
- Later becomes: Karumi (lightness) too

---

## PHASE 4: Wandering Master (1684-1694)

### The Mature Formation

**Oku-no-Hosomichi-Journey** (1689)
- Origin: 5-month walk, 1500 miles
- Grew into: Masterwork, defining text
- All assemblages peak: wandering, Zen, poetry, teaching

**Pilgrimage-to-Famous-Sites** (utamakura)
- Origin: Poetic tradition, must visit places
- Grew into: Battlefield-graves, ruined temples
- Transience theme embodied

**Sora-Companion**
- Origin: Disciple joins journey
- Grew into: Collaborative assemblage
- Later: Sora's diary counterpoint

**Patron-Network-Mature**
- Origin: Fame brings support
- Grew into: Lodging at disciples' homes
- Economic model: Arrive → teach → receive gifts → leave

**Karumi-Development** (late life)
- Origin: Rejection of both wit AND heaviness
- Grew into: Lightness, everyday, accessible
- Final aesthetic position

**Death-on-Road** (1694)
- Origin: Traveling Osaka, fell ill
- "Sick on my journey / only my dreams will wander / these desolate fields"
- Died doing what assembled him: traveling, writing

## KEY DEVELOPMENTAL PATTERNS

### 1. Master's Death as Liberation
- Yoshitada's death (1666) → masterlessness
- Grief became freedom, obligation became choice
- Without this death, no Bashō

### 2. Fire as Non-Attachment Training
- 1682 hut burns → nothing to hold
- Material loss enables spiritual wandering
- Buddhism embodied through circumstance

### 3. Class Escape via Art
- Samurai → rōnin → poet
- Poetry as social mobility mechanism
- From servant to master (of school)

### 4. Patron Economy
- Never "employed," never "independent"
- Gift economy + fame = survival
- Teaching + judging + performing = income

### 5. Fusion: Travel-Poetry-Zen
- By 1689, these aren't separate assemblages
- Walking IS meditation IS composition
- Haibun form embodies fusion
