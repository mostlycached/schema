# Areas

This page provides conceptual introductions to the major research areas currently being explored in the Hyperframes project. For detailed research materials and full schemas, visit the [GitHub repository](https://github.com/mostlycached/schema).

## Sociological & Phenomenological Worlds

An extensive framework analyzing contemporary and historical "worlds" through three simultaneous lenses:

1. **Phenomenological (Schutz)**: The subjective experience and shared meaning-making within a world
2. **Structural (Bourdieu)**: The power dynamics, capital forms, and habitus that shape behavior
3. **Systems (Luhmann)**: The functional purpose and communicative boundaries of the system

The framework encompasses over 105 distinct worlds, from novel formations like *The Algorithmic Gig Economy*, *The Twitch Livestreaming Ecosystem*, and *Reality Shifters* to long-lasting institutions like *The Monastic Order*, *The Emergency Room*, and *The Chess Club*.

Each world is characterized by:
- **Genealogy**: Historical, technological, or social origins
- **Tension**: Internal conflicts or external pressures driving evolution
- **Iteration**: Nuanced interventions that can shift the world's character
- **Inhabitants**: Key roles and actors within the system

## Attention Architecture

An architectural framework for understanding and implementing attention mechanisms, drawing on:
- Meta-cognitive attention layers
- Indexing and retrieval systems
- Critique and generation feedback loops

The architecture explores how attention operates not just as a mechanical filter, but as a multi-layered system involving scanning, meta-awareness, and dynamic indexing of experience.

## Braitenberg Vehicles & Diagrammatics

Research on Valentino Braitenberg's synthetic psychology approach, comparing it with other diagrammatic traditions (particularly Lautman's mathematical philosophy). Includes:

- Computational simulations of Braitenberg vehicles
- Narrative interpretations of vehicle behaviors (Aggressor, Coward, Explorer)
- Theoretical distinctions between different modes of diagrammatic reasoning

The work examines how simple mechanisms can generate complex, seemingly intentional behaviors ‚Äî and what this reveals about the relationship between structure and interpretation.

## Intervention & Variational Techniques

A cataloged system of techniques for generating variations on existing worlds and narratives:

- **Metaphor**: Reframing elements through unexpected comparisons
- **Noise**: Amplifying or introducing signal disruptions
- **Defamiliarization**: Making the familiar strange
- **Dialectics**: Synthesizing opposing forces
- **Technology**: Examining how tools reshape experience
- **Somatic Intervention**: Focusing on embodied, physical dimensions

These techniques are applied across domains ‚Äî from optimizing sociological worlds to generating narrative variations for audiobook production.

## Adjacent Rooms & Navigation

A theoretical framework for navigating between "pleasure rooms" ‚Äî conceptual or physical spaces that provide distinct forms of satisfaction or experience. The framework addresses:

- The "adjacent possible" ‚Äî which rooms are genuinely next steps vs. false adjacencies
- Signals for when to move (crisis, repetition, tension)
- The distinction between craft (Calvino) and being (Barthes)
